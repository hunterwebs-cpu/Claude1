The Mission

The system counts on you not understanding it.

Federal prosecutions are designed to be opaque, intimidating, and lopsided. Most defendants and their families step into it with no map, no language, and no idea how fast the most important decisions get made. We exist to change that — to put real, lived knowledge into the hands of the people who need it most, when they need it most.

Knowledge

The truth about how federal cases actually work — pretrial, plea, sentencing, and post-conviction — explained in plain language, free of the jargon that keeps people in the dark.

Strength

Information turns fear into resolve. When you understand the game being played, you stop reacting and start making the deliberate, strategic choices that protect your future.

Freedom

The goal is never just survival. It's the best possible outcome — and the dignity of facing the most frightening chapter of your life with your eyes open and your strategy clear.

Why this exists

Built by someone who lived it.

The idea behind Surviving the Feds is simple and a little defiant: people say you should hire an attorney to watch your attorney. Almost no one can afford that. So the next best thing is someone who has been through the federal system firsthand — and who came out understanding federal criminal procedure better than most who only ever studied it.

"The federal system is not built to be understood by the people caught inside it. This is the map most of them never get."

That lived experience became two books, and it's becoming something larger: a brand devoted to making sure no defendant or family ever has to walk into that courtroom blind again.
