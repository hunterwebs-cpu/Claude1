Questions families ask at 2 a.m.

Straight answers, before you buy anything.

What should I do first if a family member is arrested by federal agents?

Generally: stay calm, don't discuss the case with agents or on recorded jail lines, and find out where your family member is being held and when their initial appearance is. Everything moves fast in the first 72 hours — the detention question is usually decided within days. Start with our free First 72 Hours guide, then get informed about the process itself.

How do I find out where they took my family member?

Federal arrestees are generally held by the U.S. Marshals Service, often in a local jail or a federal detention center. The criminal docket for the initial appearance (searchable on PACER) shows the court, and the Marshals or the courthouse clerk's office can generally confirm the facility. Our free guide walks through it step by step.

Can you bail someone out of federal jail?

The federal system generally doesn't work like state cash bail. Release or detention is decided by a judge at a detention hearing, based on flight risk and danger to the community — usually through release conditions rather than a money bond you post. That hearing is often the family's single most important early moment, and the book covers how to prepare for it in depth.

What happens at a federal detention hearing?

A judge generally hears from the prosecutor and the defense about whether the defendant is a flight risk or a danger to the community, then orders release with conditions or pretrial detention. Families can matter here: stable housing, employment, and a credible third-party custodian are the kinds of factors courts generally consider. Surviving Pretrial devotes full chapters to this hearing.

Does a federal indictment mean they're going to prison?

No — an indictment is a formal charge, not a conviction. But the federal government's conviction rate is famously high, and most federal cases end in plea agreements rather than trials. That's exactly why understanding the process — what each hearing is for, how plea offers work, what the presentence report controls — changes the decisions a defendant is able to make.

How long does a federal case take?

Generally much longer than families expect — often many months, sometimes years. Continuances are routine, discovery can be enormous, and plea negotiations have their own rhythm. A long timeline is normal and is not by itself a bad sign. Knowing the stages ahead makes the waiting survivable.

How do I know if the defense lawyer is any good?

You're allowed to evaluate the lawyer — even a court-appointed one. Do they explain strategy in plain English, return calls, and prepare for the detention hearing like it matters? The book includes a full framework for vetting, hiring, and managing defense counsel. There's also a free checklist on our main site: Is my lawyer any good?

How much does a federal criminal defense attorney cost?

Private federal defense generally starts at a five-figure retainer, and complex cases or trials can cost far more — fees vary widely by district, charges, and lawyer. Whatever you spend, an informed client gets more for it: you can't judge whether counsel is worth the money if you don't know what they should be doing. That's the case for spending $25 on understanding the process before the retainer.

Should we hire a private lawyer or keep the public defender?

Not automatically — federal defenders practice in federal court every single day and are genuine specialists, while private counsel varies enormously. The real question isn't the label, it's the lawyer: do they explain strategy, prepare hard for the detention hearing, and know this courthouse? Surviving Pretrial gives you the framework to evaluate whoever is on the case before more money moves.

What is "ghost dope"?

"Ghost dope" is jailhouse shorthand for drug quantities attributed to a defendant at sentencing that were never seized — amounts based on testimony or estimates rather than drugs on a table. Because federal drug sentences are driven by quantity, those paper amounts can raise a sentence dramatically. The book explains how ghost dope shows up in the presentence report and how it's generally challenged.
