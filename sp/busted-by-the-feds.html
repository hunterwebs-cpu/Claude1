An honest comparison

Busted by the Feds: the classic jailhouse manual — and the book that picks up where it left off.

If you're searching for Busted by the Feds, someone you trust probably told you to. That was good advice for a long time. Here's what's changed, laid out fairly — checkable facts and real reviews, no trash talk.

First, respect where it's due

Busted by the Feds earned its reputation. Larry Fassler wrote it after years of helping fellow inmates with their cases, it ran through more than twenty editions, and for decades it was the book that got passed hand-to-hand around county jails — often photocopied, because copies were scarce and expensive. Plenty of people got real help from it. That deserves to be said plainly.

But two things have changed, and if you're making this decision tonight, you deserve to know both.

What changed

The author is gone. Larry Fassler passed away in federal custody. The 2023 edition appears to be the last, there is no website, and there is no one behind the book to update it or answer a question. The federal system, meanwhile, keeps changing.

The price puts it out of reach. Editions of Busted by the Feds are commonly listed on Amazon for well over $100 — often $150 and up. That's the reason for the jailhouse photocopy culture in the first place. For a family already paying lawyers, commissary, and phone calls, that's a real barrier on the worst week of your life.

Readers over the years have also noted that its examples lean heavily on drug cases, which can leave defendants in other kinds of cases translating as they read.

Where Surviving Pretrial picks up

Surviving Pretrial was written by Bilal Khan, who went through the federal gauntlet himself — arrest, detention, the plea decision, the presentence report, sentencing — and taught law to fellow inmates along the way. It covers the full pretrial sequence in plain English, for the defendant and for the family carrying the case from the outside:

  • Vetting, hiring, and managing a defense lawyer — and telling whether they're actually fighting
  • The detention hearing: proving someone isn't a flight risk or a danger
  • Every hearing decoded: initial appearance, arraignment, preliminary, suppression
  • Plea agreements: the types, the discretionary-language traps, the Safety Valve
  • The PSR and "ghost dope" — challenging drug amounts that were never seized
  • Sentencing, and everything a family can do before it

It costs what a book should cost: $24.99 on Kindle, free with Kindle Unlimited, and normal paperback pricing — which Amazon can ship directly into a detention facility.
