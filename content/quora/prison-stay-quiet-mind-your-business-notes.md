## Brain dump — raw, Round 1 (paused mid-thought, more to come)

Tough question, because it is part right and part wrong. Ultimately, if you
want to survive prison you have to think of yourself in terms of a financial
statement. You are either an asset to the inmate community or a liability.
There are fights, and sometimes people who mind their business and stay quiet
get roped into shit.

**Key one:** you gotta hold your posture right. Head up, posture straight,
shoulders back. How you hold yourself will dictate how you are treated. Guys
who hold their heads down like in shame become easy targets for opportunistic
assholes who want to rob and steal from them.

**Key two:** it's not "mind your business" — we call it **stay in your lane.**
Worry about the traffic in front of you and behind you, but don't worry about
what that traffic is doing inside their cars. You're going to see guys do
drugs, use cell phones, have fights, even have sex. None of that is your
business. But simply "minding your own business" isn't enough. Don't look in
other people's cells/rooms/bunk areas. If you aren't in that person's circle,
keep your face forward. If you're "minding your business" but looking at
people, you'll get smashed in a lot of institutions.

So you need to be mindful of your surroundings. Everyone is paying attention
to what you're doing, and you need to be aware of what others are doing THAT
AFFECTS YOU — while also not looking at what anyone is doing that doesn't
bother you. The ultimate rule: you're "bothering" someone if you're looking at
them do things that are against the rules or outright illegal.

Doing illegal things yourself, within reason (smoke a cigarette, drink some
liquor, use a cell phone), will help other inmates trust you more — but that's
not something you have to do. A lot of guys keep to themselves and immerse
themselves in religion, sports, even music. But those guys are routinely
looked at like they're going to snitch.

You have to get thick skin. When people have a lot of time and little to do,
they gossip. Gossip can get you in trouble in prison, but that doesn't stop
various groups from doing it.

Prison is a lot like starting a new junior high. Everyone is sizing you up,
and you'll try to find your place. Keep your head up, don't cower, look people
in the eye when you talk to them — while being respectful and not paying any
attention to what they're doing unless it involves or directly affects you —
and you'll be fine.
