# In re Administrative Remedy of Anibal Soto, Reg. No. 67133-054 (FCI Fort Dix, NJ)

## Matter Summary

BP-9 administrative remedy (federal inmate grievance, 28 C.F.R. §§ 542.10–542.19) challenging
BOP staff's refusal to submit Anibal Soto for Second Chance Act ("SCA") Residential
Reentry Center ("RRC") / halfway house placement.

## Timeline

- **March 17, 2026**: Case Manager Dennis presented Soto with RRC referral paperwork for
  signature. The paperwork listed Soto as "homeless." Soto refused to sign the document
  as presented because it contained a false statement of fact, gave Dennis his actual home
  address in New York, and asked that the paperwork be corrected. Soto did not refuse RRC
  placement or consideration itself — he refused to attest to false information on a
  federal document.
- **July 3, 2026**: At Soto's formal team meeting, Dennis informed Soto for the first time
  that the March 17 refusal-to-sign-false-document incident was being treated as a
  categorical refusal of RRC placement, and that Soto was "no longer being considered for
  RRC placement." Soto submitted a BP-8 (informal resolution) the same day.
- **July 6, 2026**: BP-8 delivered to counselor.
- **July 15, 2026**: BP-8 response issued by Dennis, asserting (a) the operative incident
  date is March 17, 2026, not July 3, 2026, and (b) under PS 1330.18, Soto had only 20 days
  from March 17 to initiate informal resolution, making the BP-8 untimely. Dennis crossed
  out Soto's stated incident date (7-3-2026) and substituted "March 17, 2026."
- Fort Dix institutional supplement (Warden Thompson) requires BP-8 responses within 3
  working days of submission. The July 15 response — 9 calendar days (7 working days) after
  the 7/6 submission — is itself untimely under that supplement.

## Client's Position

1. **Timeliness**: The operative "incident" for administrative-remedy purposes is not the
   March 17 refusal to sign a false document (which was not itself communicated as a
   categorical denial of RRC consideration) — it is the July 3, 2026 notification that
   BOP was treating that refusal as grounds to permanently remove Soto from RRC
   consideration. The 20-day clock under PS 1330.18 runs from July 3. In the alternative,
   this is a continuing/ongoing denial of statutorily-required consideration, not a single
   discrete "incident," so no limitations clock forecloses relief. BOP's own response was
   also untimely under the Fort Dix supplement (3 working days), which independently should
   not be held against Soto and undercuts any argument that strict timeliness rules should
   bar his claim.
2. **Merits**: Even accepting Dennis's version of events, an inmate's refusal to attest to
   false information on a federal document is not a lawful basis to categorically deny SCA
   RRC consideration. Congress's grant of RRC placement authority in 18 U.S.C. § 3624(c)
   and 34 U.S.C. § 60541 confines BOP discretion to the length/conditions of placement
   (subject to the 34 U.S.C. § 60541(g) individualized-determination factors), not to a
   threshold gatekeeping power to exclude inmates from consideration altogether for reasons
   untethered to the statutory factors. There is no BOP policy giving staff authority to (i)
   remove an inmate from RRC consideration entirely, or (ii) condition consideration on an
   inmate's willingness to sign a document containing information he disputes as false.
