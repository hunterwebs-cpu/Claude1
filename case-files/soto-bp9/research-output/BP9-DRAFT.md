## ATTACHMENT PAGE (the one allowed continuation sheet)

TIMELINESS — Basis for this Request is 7/3/26, when Dennis first told me the 3/17/26 refusal-to-sign was being treated as a permanent bar; nothing adverse happened on 3/17/26 itself. 28 C.F.R. § 542.14(a) / PS 1330.18 § 8.a run the 20-day clock from "the date on which the basis for the Request occurred," not an earlier event that caused no injury — I filed same day I learned of the adverse decision. Even if the clock is measured from 3/17/26: (1) § 542.16(a) / PS § 10 excuse delay "caused by staff" — Dennis's own 4.5-month silence is staff delay; (2) § 542.14(b) / PS § 8.b list "an unusually long period... for informal resolution" as valid delay — the Fort Dix supplement (Warden Thompson) requires a BP-8 response within 3 working days, Dennis took 9 calendar days; (3) Ross v. Blake, 578 U.S. 632 (2016), and Robinson v. Supt. Rockview SCI, 831 F.3d 148, 154 (3d Cir. 2016), hold an institution's own failure to meet its procedural deadlines makes a remedy "unavailable" and cannot be held against the inmate.

MERITS — 18 U.S.C. § 3624(c)(6)(A) requires RRC placement be "determined on an individual basis" and "consistent with § 3621(b)"; the five § 3621(b) factors (facility resources, offense, prisoner history, sentencing-court statement, USSC policy) do not include declining to sign disputed paperwork. Woodall v. FBOP, 432 F.3d 235 (3d Cir. 2005) (binding, D.N.J.), holds BOP cannot substitute a categorical bar for individualized § 3621(b) review. PS 7310.04 § 12: only the Warden has final RRC referral authority, not a case manager. PS 7310.04 § 11: a genuine "refusal" requires staff investigation plus a memo signed by both the Associate Warden (Programs) and the inmate — none of that occurred because I never refused placement.

RELIEF — Rescind the untimely determination and the "no longer being considered" decision; order a prompt individualized SCA/RRC review under § 3621(b) without regard to the 3/17/26 document dispute; correct my Central File.
