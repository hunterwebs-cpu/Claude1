## PART A — INMATE REQUEST

I am submitting this Request for Administrative Remedy (BP-9) because Case Manager Dennis is refusing to submit me for Second Chance Act ("SCA") Residential Reentry Center ("RRC")/halfway house placement, and because his July 15, 2026 response to my BP-8 incorrectly rejects that request as untimely. I request that both the timeliness determination and the underlying refusal to consider me for RRC placement be reversed.

### 1. Statement of Facts

My GCT release date is October 28, 2027. I was previously recommended for full Second Chance Act RRC placement. On March 17, 2026, Case Manager Dennis presented me with RRC referral paperwork to sign. That paperwork listed me as "homeless." That was false. I have a home in New York, and I gave Case Manager Dennis my actual home address that same day and asked that the paperwork be corrected to reflect it. I refused to sign the document *as written* because it contained a false statement of fact on a federal document — I did not refuse RRC placement, and I did not refuse to cooperate with the referral process. Every other part of the paperwork was accurate.

I heard nothing further about this until my team meeting on July 3, 2026. At that meeting, Case Manager Dennis told me for the first time that he considered my March 17 refusal to sign the inaccurate document to be a "refusal" of RRC placement itself, and that I was "no longer being considered" for any RRC placement at all. This was the first time anyone communicated to me that my March 17 request to correct false information would be treated as a permanent bar to RRC consideration. I submitted my BP-8 that same day, July 3, 2026, and turned it in to my counselor on July 6, 2026.

On July 15, 2026, Case Manager Dennis provided a written response asserting that the "incident" occurred on March 17, 2026, not July 3, 2026, and that under Program Statement 1330.18 I had only 20 days from March 17 to complete informal resolution — meaning, in his view, my BP-8 was untimely by several months. He crossed out the date I had written on the form (7-3-2026) and wrote "March 17, 2026" beside it. His response also asserts, for the first time in writing, that I "adamantly refused" the RRC paperwork and stated I would "rather finish [my] sentence here in prison" — I dispute that characterization; what happened is what I describe above.

I also note that the Fort Dix institutional supplement to PS 1330.18, issued by Warden Thompson, requires a response to a BP-8 within 3 working days of submission. My BP-8 was submitted to my counselor on July 6, 2026, and was not answered until July 15, 2026 — 9 calendar days later, well beyond the institution's own 3-working-day standard.

### 2. Timeliness of This Request

This Request is timely under 28 C.F.R. § 542.14(a) and PS 1330.18 § 8.a, which set the 20-calendar-day clock running from "the date on which the basis for the Request occurred" — not from any earlier event that produced no adverse action. The basis for this Request is Case Manager Dennis's decision to permanently remove me from RRC consideration, which he did not communicate to me until my team meeting on July 3, 2026. On March 17, 2026, nothing adverse happened to me that I could have grieved: I corrected inaccurate information on a federal document and asked that the referral proceed with accurate information. I had no reason to believe, and was not told, that doing so would end my RRC candidacy. I filed my BP-8 the same day I learned of the adverse decision. This Request is therefore timely on its face.

Even if the institution takes the position that the clock should be measured from March 17, 2026, that lateness is excused for two independent reasons under BOP's own regulations:

First, 28 C.F.R. § 542.16(a) and PS 1330.18 § 10 state that a delay in filing will be excused where "the delay was caused by staff." Any delay here was caused by Case Manager Dennis, who did not disclose until July 3, 2026 that he was treating the March 17 event as a categorical bar to RRC consideration. I cannot be charged with failing to timely grieve a decision I was not told about.

Second, 28 C.F.R. § 542.14(b) and PS 1330.18 § 8.b list "an unusually long period taken for informal resolution attempts" as an enumerated valid reason for delay. The nearly four-and-a-half-month gap between March 17 and July 3 — during which nothing was communicated to me about how the March 17 incident would be treated — is exactly that kind of delay, and it is BOP's, not mine.

I also note that BOP's own delay in responding to my BP-8 (9 days against the Fort Dix supplement's 3-working-day standard) independently undermines any strict application of timeliness rules against me here. An institution that does not meet its own procedural deadlines should not be permitted to enforce a rigid deadline against the inmate for a decision the institution itself failed to timely communicate.

### 3. The Merits — BOP's Discretion Over RRC Placement Is Not Unlimited

Even setting timeliness aside, the underlying decision to remove me from RRC consideration is not a lawful exercise of BOP's discretion.

Congress requires that placement in a community corrections facility be "conducted in a manner consistent with section 3621(b)" and "determined on an individual basis." 18 U.S.C. § 3624(c)(6)(A)–(B). Section 3621(b) sets out the factors BOP may consider in making that determination: the resources of the facility, the nature and circumstances of the offense, the history and characteristics of the prisoner, any statement by the sentencing court, and any pertinent Sentencing Commission policy statement, along with bed availability, security designation, programmatic and medical needs, and other security concerns. Nowhere on that list is "the inmate declined to sign a referral document containing information he disputed as false." That is not one of the considerations Congress authorized BOP to weigh, and it is not a lawful basis to remove an inmate from consideration altogether.

BOP's own Program Statement 7310.04 confirms this. Under Section 12, the unit team — which includes the case manager — only *recommends* CCC/RRC referrals; **the Warden is the final decision-making authority**. Under Section 11, a genuine "refusal" of CCC/RRC placement requires staff to investigate the inmate's stated reasons, and requires a memorandum, **signed by both the Associate Warden (Programs) and the inmate**, documenting the inmate's rationale and the unit team's efforts to encourage participation. None of that occurred here. I was never asked to sign any refusal memorandum, no Associate Warden was involved, and no one investigated my actual reason for declining to sign the March 17 paperwork — which was that it was factually false, not that I did not want RRC placement. Section 10 of the same Program Statement lists the only categories of inmates who may be categorically excluded from CCC/RRC programs, and disputing inaccurate information on a referral form is not among them; even the closest category on that list requires a Warden-signed memorandum explaining the rationale for exclusion.

In short, BOP's own governing policy does not authorize a case manager, acting alone, to unilaterally and permanently remove an inmate from all future RRC consideration for declining to attest to information he disputes as inaccurate. What happened here does not meet BOP's own definition of a "refusal," was never elevated to the Warden as required for a final referral decision or a categorical exclusion, and was never documented in the manner BOP's own policy requires.

### 4. Requested Relief

I respectfully request that this Administrative Remedy be granted, and specifically that:

1. Case Manager Dennis's determination that my BP-8 was untimely be reversed, and this Request be accepted and processed on the merits;
2. Case Manager Dennis's determination that I am "no longer being considered" for RRC/Second Chance Act placement be rescinded;
3. My unit team conduct a prompt, individualized review of my SCA RRC placement, applying the factors set out in 18 U.S.C. § 3621(b), without reference to the March 17, 2026 paperwork dispute, and that I be submitted for full SCA RRC placement as previously recommended;
4. My Central File be corrected to reflect my accurate home address in New York and to remove any "homeless" designation; and
5. If the institution nonetheless maintains that a "refusal" of placement occurred, that the investigation-and-signed-memorandum procedure required by PS 7310.04 § 11 be followed before any refusal is recorded, so that my actual stated reason (the false "homeless" designation) is part of the record.
