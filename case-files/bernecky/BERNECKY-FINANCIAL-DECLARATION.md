## Note on the CJA 23 form

Separately, have Mr. Bernecky complete the standard **CJA Form 23 (Financial Affidavit)** — the form federal courts use under 18 U.S.C. § 3006A to assess eligibility for appointed counsel. Reasons to do both rather than just one:

- The CJA 23 is the form the Court and Probation already recognize and expect; a court-recognized form carries weight the custom declaration above doesn't have on its own.
- The custom declaration above captures case-specific facts the CJA 23's generic categories won't surface on their own — the fee-stacking problem, the furnishing cost, the work-stoppage timing, and the direct tie to Special Conditions (a), (b), (g), (h).
- If a counsel-appointment question ever resurfaces (the earlier, shelved CJA-appointment theory for the polygraph track), a CJA 23 already on file removes a step.

The CJA 23 form itself should be obtained directly from the court (uscourts.gov or the Clerk's Office for W.D.N.Y.) rather than reconstructed here, since it's a fillable federal form with a fixed format — I'd rather not risk transcribing it inaccurately.
