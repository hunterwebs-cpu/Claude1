### Argument 1: "Brad has zero income and cannot sustain himself — not a strategic filer"

**Schedule I shows:** Brad is not employed. His only income is $3,500/month in support from his brother Courtney Mell. His monthly expenses are $4,552/month, leaving a deficit of -$1,052/month. Brad's own schedules establish that he is financially dependent on a family member for basic survival — this is not the profile of a strategic debtor using bankruptcy as a tactical weapon.

**SOFA confirms:** Brad had NO income from employment or business operations during the current year or the prior two calendar years. He earned nothing. He received nothing from Social Security, disability, or government programs.

**The Means Test result confirms financial distress:** Brad's current monthly income of $0.00 (for Means Test purposes) is dramatically below the NJ median income of $87,173/year. There is no presumption of abuse. The Means Test — the very mechanism Congress designed to filter out strategic Chapter 7 filers — found no abuse.

**Attorney's fees paid by brother:** Even the $5,000 bankruptcy retainer was paid by Brad's brother Courtney, not by Brad. Brad has no independent financial resources whatsoever.
