## Core Factual Finding

The policy did NOT vanish. Nonpayment of the January 2022 premium triggered the policy's
own **Contingent Nonforfeiture Protection Endorsement** (Part of Policy, form LTC-99CN
4/99 / QCN96G), which — because the policy had been in force >3 years and the cumulative
premium increase crossed the age-based statutory/contractual threshold — automatically
converted the policy to **paid-up status with a reduced Policy Limit equal to total
premiums paid ($70,845.71)** rather than terminating coverage outright. This is confirmed
by every annual statement from 1/2023 through 1/2026. The daily benefit ($525/day) is
unchanged; only the total lifetime pool and benefit period changed. This paid-up benefit
is presently in force and claimable.

Separately, the policy contains **§5.4 "Added Protection Against Lapse"**, a distinct and
more powerful reinstatement right: if the insured would have qualified for benefits (but
for the elimination period) on the date of termination, the policy "may be reinstated"
(implicitly to FULL pre-lapse status, not just the CBUL paid-up amount) within 5 months of
termination, upon proof of eligibility and payment of overdue premiums. This window has
facially expired (termination ~April 2022 + 5 months = ~September 2022), but:

- No document in the file shows John Hancock actually gave the §5.2-required written
  notice of non-payment "to You and the person or persons You designate" with termination
  effective "30 days from the date that notice... is given." No third-party designee was
  ever on file ("NO DESIGNEE SELECTED" on every notice), despite the policy requiring
  John Hancock to "provide You with a reminder of the right to change this written
  designation every two years."
- If Pamela had a cognitive impairment (as defined in §2.2) or functional incapacity in
  the relevant window, and notice was defective or she lacked capacity to act on it, there
  may be equitable, statutory, or regulatory grounds (independent of the strict 5-month
  contract clause) to seek full reinstatement now.
