## Note on the Underlying Civil Case Against Mell

Before turning to comparative NJ cases, the research surfaced directly relevant prior litigation:

**B.B. v. S. Bradley Mell**, Docket No. A-3450-19 (N.J. App. Div. 2020), reported at 465 N.J. Super. 331, 243 A.3d 680.

- **Facts:** Over five months (July–December 2017), Mell engaged in sexual relations with plaintiff B.B., then 15 years old. Mell was arrested May 2018 and pleaded guilty to state and federal crimes arising from victimizing B.B. He is serving a seven-year federal prison term.
- **Civil Action:** B.B. commenced civil action for damages in October 2019 and obtained a prejudgment writ of attachment against Mell's assets and income, including business interests (Gulfstream and Aero Care) and retirement distributions.
- **Appellate Holdings:** The Appellate Division ruled in favor of B.B. (i) that B.B. had priority over the attached funds superior to Mell and his counsel, and (ii) that counsel fees could not be paid from attached funds.
- **Subsequent proceedings:** A-2990-21 (App. Div. 2024, unpublished); A-3582-24 (App. Div. 2026) — continued litigation, including a legal malpractice action by Mell against his former civil attorneys.
- **Damages amount in B.B.'s civil case:** Not publicly reported in available secondary sources. The civil damages case remains relevant to the creditor claim in the bankruptcy. ## Summary Analysis for $10 Million Demand Contextualization

### Range of NJ Awards — Key Data Points

**High end (institutional defendants, child abuse, multi-year/multi-perpetrator):**
- $30M verdict (Newark BOE, single child plaintiff, 5 years of abuse, 2026)
- $25M verdict (State/DYFS, single child plaintiff, 3 foster homes/4 perpetrators, 2024)
- $19.5M settlement (2 siblings, foster care abuse spanning years, 2025)
- $12M high-low (State, single adult plaintiff, 2024)
- $8M settlement (State/foster care, single plaintiff, 2026)
- $6.75M settlement (State/foster care, single adult plaintiff, 2024)

**Mid-range (institutional defendants, child abuse, single incident or shorter duration):**
- $6M settlement — 2 plaintiffs ($3M each), school district, Winslow Twp. (2024)
- $5M verdict — single plaintiff, Delbarton/religious order, single assault age 15 (2025)
- $3.5M settlement — single plaintiff, state developmental center, years of abuse (2024)
- $3M settlement — single plaintiff, school district, Edison (2024)

**Lower range (institutional, limited abuse, or smaller districts):**
- $2M settlement — school district, Camden (2024)
- $1.6M verdict — school district, Camden (2024)
- $1.25M settlement — school, single adult plaintiff, Rosa Parks School (2025)
- $1M settlement — State, adult victim, probation officer sextortion
- $750K verdict — State, child abuse, Salem County juvenile facility (2023–24)
- $750K settlement — State, Monmouth County child treatment center (2024)
- $650K settlement — school district, Burlington County (2025)
- $331K verdict — municipal/fire dept., child abuse (2023)
- $125K settlement — school district, Ocean County (2022)

### Observations Relevant to the Mell Bankruptcy Claim

1. **$10 million is well within the NJ range for serious child sexual abuse by an individual** where institutional negligence is not present. The $5M Delbarton verdict (single plaintiff, single incident in 1975–76, no punitive damages) demonstrates that NJ juries will award substantial sums even without institutional negligence—against a religious order with deep pockets. For a private individual defendant like Mell, without an institutional co-defendant, verdicts tend to be lower due to collectability concerns, but the claim value is not so constrained.

2. **Institutional cases dominate the high end.** The $25M–$30M verdicts involved state agencies and public school districts. Where the defendant is an individual—as Mell is—jury verdicts and settlements for single-plaintiff child sexual abuse cases in NJ appear to cluster in the $1M–$6M range (non-institutional), though the Delbarton $5M verdict demonstrates the range can go higher even for a single victim and single incident.

3. **Duration and repetition matter significantly.** The larger awards (e.g., $25M, $30M) involved sustained abuse over years across multiple perpetrators. Mell's abuse of B.B. over five months is more limited in duration but still involved a position-of-trust/extortion dynamic. The $6M Winslow ($3M per plaintiff) and $5M Delbarton awards are the most apt comparators for a single victim, single perpetrator, institutional or quasi-institutional context.

4. **B.B.'s civil case against Mell has not yielded a publicly reported final damages amount.** The prejudgment attachment proceedings confirm significant anticipated recovery. A $10M demand in the bankruptcy context appears aggressive relative to the NJ single-plaintiff, individual-defendant comparable cases but is consistent with the general range for serious child sexual abuse cases in NJ where the abuse involved an adult in authority exploiting a 15-year-old over multiple months.
